Product Launch April 2026

Chainage v1.2 — Guided Tour, Draw-to-Create & Grid Overhaul

Our biggest UX update yet. Chainage v1.2 ships with a 9-step interactive guided tour that walks new users through every key feature — from adding their first activity to running a Monte Carlo simulation — with spotlight highlights directly on the real interface.

Also new: draw-to-create. Press D on the canvas, drag diagonally across the chart, and a new activity is created at exactly the dates and chainages you drew. This is how time-chainage planning should work — spatial and intuitive, not form-driven.

The grid view has been completely reorganised. 9 core columns are visible by default (P6 ID, Name, Start, End, Ch Start, Ch End, Shape, Colour, Groups). Advanced columns — resource loading, costs, opacity, outline — are hidden by default but accessible via the new ⊞ Columns picker. Colour cells now open a palette of 28 infrastructure-appropriate colours instead of requiring hex input.

Industry March 2026

Why time-chainage beats Gantt for linear infrastructure

Most planning tools are built for building construction — where location doesn't matter. For roads, rail, tunnels, and pipelines, location is everything. A Gantt chart tells you when Activity A happens. A time-chainage chart tells you when and where — and critically, at what production rate.

The slope of a line on a time-chainage chart represents production rate. Parallel lines mean activities are running at the same pace — useful for showing chase sequences where one trade follows another at a defined offset. You can see zone clashes, resource conflicts, and schedule compression at a glance in a way that a Gantt simply cannot show.

This is why every major UK infrastructure project — HS2, Lower Thames Crossing, East West Rail — uses time-chainage as the primary planning tool for linear works. And it's why we built Chainage.

Launch February 2026

Chainage is live — the browser-based alternative to TILOS

After months of development, Chainage is publicly available. It started as a response to a simple frustration: the best time-chainage tools cost thousands per seat, require installation, and carry steep learning curves. GraphicScheduler and similar Excel-based tools are accessible but fundamentally limited — no scheduling engine, no risk analysis, no resource loading.

Chainage fills the gap. It runs in any browser with no installation, includes a full CPM scheduler, Monte Carlo risk analysis, resource histogram, float heatmap, and zone occupancy clash detection — all in one tool at a fraction of the cost of enterprise alternatives.
